3.1 ESP32 Primary Pins
U1 (ESP32-S3-WROOM-1-N16R8), the central MCU. Fans out I2C (I2C_SDA/SCL) to the accelerometer, NFC tag, and both IO expanders, SPI (SPI_MOSI/MISO/CLK) to the display and SD card, the dedicated camera DVP bus (C_DATA0-9, C_VSYNC, C_HREF, C_PCLK, C_XCLK), UART (RXD0/TXD0), and a JTAG header for debug. SW2 and SW3 (TS5215A) sit on EN and IO0 respectively, giving hardware reset and boot mode buttons.
3.2 USB Connector and ESD Protection
USB4105-GF-A-060 USB-C receptacle providing USB_DP/DN and VBUS. USBLC6-2SC6 clamps the raw D+/D- lines (USB_DP_RAW/USB_DN_RAW) before they reach the ESP32's native USB pins, protecting the exposed connector against ESD.
3.3 Battery Voltage Regulation
Q3 (AO3401A, P-channel MOSFET) load switches VBAT onto the +VSYS rail, letting the battery be gated off downstream when not needed.
3.4 Charging Chip
U2 (MCP73833-AMI/MF) linear LiPo charger. R4 is an NTC thermistor on the THERM pin for temperature qualified charging. STAT1/STAT2 drive LED9/LED10 through R3 (1k) as charge status indicators.
3.5 5V Rail
U9 (SX1308) boost converter steps VSYS up to +5V, using L2 (4.7uH), D2/D3 (SS14 Schottky) rectifiers, R10/R11/R21 feedback divider, and C25/C26 (22uF) output filtering.
3.6 3V3 Rail
U7 (AP63203WU-7) buck converter drops VSYS/5V to +3V3, using L1 (6.8uH) and C10/C14 (22uF) filtering. Powers the ESP32 and most digital peripherals.
3.7 NFC 1V3 and 2V8 Rails
Two linear LDOs isolate the NFC analog front end from the digital 3V3 rail. U14 (AP2120N-1.3) outputs +1V3, U17 (AP2125K-2.8) outputs +2V8, both sourced from +3V3. These feed the NT3H2211W0FHKH NFC IC directly, with LA/LB antenna pads broken out to a 2-pin header (HEAD2) for the loop antenna.
3.8 IO Expander #1 (Peripherals)
PCA9554APW on the I2C bus, adding 8 GPIOs for peripheral enable and reset lines that don't need to consume native ESP32 pins.
3.9 IO Expander #2 (Switches)
Second PCA9554APW reading the SW_R0-3/SW_C0-2 button matrix lines over the same I2C bus, offloading button scanning from the ESP32.
3.10 Tactile Button Matrix
9x PTS645SH50SMTR92LFS (SW7-SW15) in a 3x3 row and column grid. Diodes D6-D14 prevent ghosting on simultaneous key presses. R8/R9/R14-16/R19/R22-29 (10k) hold rows and columns at defined idle levels.
3.11 Backup Button
SW1 (MSK12C02-HB), pulled up via R7 (100k), on a dedicated Backup net, separate hardware input independent of the main matrix.
3.12 Accelerometer
U13 (LIS3DHTR) on I2C, with INT1/INT2 routed to GPIO_INT1/GPIO_INT2 for tap detection and orientation sensing. This is what detects the 90 degree rotation that triggers camera mode.
3.13 Camera Connector
FFC2B28-24-G (24-pin FPC) carries the OV2640's DVP bus (C_DATA0-9, VSYNC, HREF, PCLK, XCLK), I2C/SCCB config, CAM_EN power gate, and CAM_RST.
3.14 Display Connector
FH28K-40S-0.5SH (40-pin FPC) carries SPI (LCD_SCL/LCD_SDA), LCD_DC, LCD_CS, D_RESET, and D_BL_EN to the ILI9341V TFT. U15 (AP3032KTR-G1) is the backlight boost converter.
3.15 Neopixels
8x XL-2020RGBC-2812B (LED1-8) plus 1x WS2812B-B/T (LED11), chained on NEO_DIN and buffered through U6 (74AHCT1G125GV, 3.3V to 5V) to meet WS2812 signal timing. Q1 (AO3400A) gates power via NEO_EN.
3.16 Buzzer
Piezo (BUZZER1, 2kHz) driven differentially by U10 (PAM8302AASCR) class-D amp, fed by BZ_PWM.
3.17 IR Blaster
U16 (940nm IR LED) driven by Q6 (MMBT2222A) switched via IR_TX through R18 (1k) base resistor, with R25/R17 (100R) current limiting.
3.18 Laser
R20 (2.49R) sets current limit on the LSR_EN controlled laser driver path.
3.19 SD Card Hold/Read
DM3AT-SF-PEJM5 microSD holder providing DAT0-3, CMD, CLK, VDD/VSS, plus SD_CD (card detect) and SD_CS for SPI access.
